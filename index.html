How Arbitrage Betting Works

Arbitrage betting involves placing bets on both sides of a game at different sportsbooks when there are price discrepancies, ensuring a profit regardless of the outcome. Though profits can be small and opportunities hard to find, using arbitrage betting software simplifies the process.

Examples of Arbitrage Betting

For instance, if DraftKings offers the Yankees at +110 and the Red Sox at -105, and FanDuel offers the reverse odds, betting $100 on each side guarantees a profit. By betting on both the Yankees and Red Sox at opposite sportsbooks, you lock in a return regardless of the outcome.

How to Find Arbitrage Opportunities

Identifying favorable pricing requires constant monitoring of multiple sportsbooks. Comparing odds, watching line movements, and taking advantage of promotions or free bets can also reveal arbitrage opportunities.
